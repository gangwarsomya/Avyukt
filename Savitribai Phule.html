Savitribai Phule

(1831-1897)



Savitribai Phule was a Maharashtrian poet, social reformer, and the country's first female teacher. She made a significant contribution to advancing women's rights in India along with her husband, Jyotiba Phule in Maharashtra.

She is credited with founding the feminist movement in India. In Pune, at Bhidewada, Savitribai and Jyotiba founded one of the first early modern Indian girls' schools in 1848. She worked to end prejudice and unjust treatment of people based on their gender and caste.

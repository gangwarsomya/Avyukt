Lal Ded

(1320-1392)



Lal Ded (Mother Lalla), also known as Lalla or Lalleshwari, was a Kashmiri saint and mystic poet who was born in modern-day Kashmir in the 14th century. She was married at the age of 12 into a family who allegedly mistreated her on a regular basis. She gave up her material life and marriage after becoming Sidh Srikanth's disciple in order to devote herself to the god Shiva. She was a mystic who wandered naked, reciting her quatrain-based poetry and proverbs. Lal Ded frequently used poetry as a nonviolent way to interact with both Shaivism and Sufism.

Numerous people have translated her poetry, including Jane Hirshfield, Coleman Barks, and K.C.I.E. Sir George Grierson for Women in Praise of the Sacred: 43 Centuries of Spiritual Poetry by Women (1994), Naked Song: Lalla (1992), and Lalla-Vakyani or The Wise Sayings of Lal-Ded, A Mystic Poetess of Ancient Kashmir (1920).
